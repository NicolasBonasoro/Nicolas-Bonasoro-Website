Hi! I'm Nic 👋

I am an Aerospace Engineering student at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University with a background in business and a focus on space robotics, physics-based simulation, lunar terramechanics, and hardware integration.

Over the past year, I have worked on research projects in the Space Robotics & Generative Estimation Lab at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in Daytona Beach, Florida. My work has included NASA Florida Space Grant Consortium-supported lunar trafficability research, rover payload development, deformable terrain simulation, and robust autonomous lunar landing studies.

My engineering interests center on building, simulating, and testing systems that support future lunar surface operations, including rover mobility, regolith interaction, landing stability, and infrastructure that helps humans operate safely on the Moon.
